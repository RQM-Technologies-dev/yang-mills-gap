# Problem Statement

The Clay Yang-Mills existence and mass gap problem asks for a rigorous construction of quantum Yang-Mills theory on four-dimensional spacetime for a compact simple gauge group, together with proof of a positive mass gap.

This repository does not attempt to prove that result. It builds a computational and conceptual sandbox around SU(2) lattice Yang-Mills, using unit quaternions as coordinates for SU(2) link variables.

The central exploratory thesis is:

> "The Yang–Mills mass gap may be interpreted in RQM as the lowest nonzero energy of a closed, gauge-invariant non-abelian curvature resonance."

The first target is modest and standard:

- represent SU(2) link variables as unit quaternions,
- compute plaquettes and the Wilson action,
- preserve local gauge invariance,
- measure basic gauge-invariant observables,
- build a disciplined path for later interpretive or deformed experiments.

Finite-lattice numerical behavior is not a continuum theorem. Any future proof-oriented document must identify the missing analytical steps explicitly.
