# Closure Resonance Interpretation

The closure defect:

```text
D_p = 1 - scalar_part(U_p)
```

is a local gauge-invariant scalar extracted from a closed plaquette. It measures how far the plaquette holonomy is from the identity in the scalar SU(2) trace channel.

The RQM interpretation treats gauge-invariant closed curvature structures as possible resonance carriers. In that vocabulary, a glueball-like operator built from spatial plaquettes can be read as a closed non-abelian curvature excitation.

This interpretation is deliberately downstream of the standard lattice construction:

- first compute standard plaquette observables,
- then form gauge-invariant operators,
- then study correlators,
- only then discuss resonance language.

The phrase:

> "The Yang–Mills mass gap may be interpreted in RQM as the lowest nonzero energy of a closed, gauge-invariant non-abelian curvature resonance."

should be read as a hypothesis about interpretation, not as a completed theorem.
